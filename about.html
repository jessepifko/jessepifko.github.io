About Me

Bio

I grew up in a suburb of Detroit called Warren. My childhood was spent running around outside with the neighborhood boys, learning about computers, and playing video games. I attended high school at Center Line High in Center Line, Michigan where I played football. Some of my fondest memories of high school were either on the football field or the computer science labs. It was here where I learned how to build websites from the ground up using HTML and CSS.

My first high school job was as a dishwasher in a catering hall on the weekends. Junior year I got a job as an assistant butcher where I learned how to prep the meat counters and how to use the machines. After graduation, I started out attending night school at Baker College while working during the day. This time was especially challenging and was exhausting, especially after a long day. But I persevered and after a year, I decided to transfer to University. At university, I got involved in student affairs and business plan competitions through the College of Business and Entrepreneurship.

After graduation, I spent a few years in sales in different capacities selling gym memberships, consumer electronics, and used vehicles. Sales was a means to an end while discovering my real passion. I found Grand Circus in the midst of that search and decided to jump in feet first. Starting in April of 2020, I dedicated myself to learn the principles and foundations of building software using C# and the .NET framework. I have spent roughly 60 hours per week throughout the 12 weeks working on homework and assigned projects. I could not be more proud of myself for passing and cannot wait to start out as a junior developer in the industry.
